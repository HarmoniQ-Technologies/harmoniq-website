Power Quality for Ports & Shipping

Crane drives, shore power connections, and reefer containers are pushing port electrical networks — and your energy bill — to the limit.

5 min read © 2026 HarmoniQ Technologies

The Problem

Ports Are Electrically Extreme Environments

Container terminals concentrate some of the most demanding electrical loads in any commercial operation. Ship-to-shore cranes regenerate energy during hoist-down cycles, creating voltage spikes. Cold ironing connections deliver 1–16 MVA per vessel with massive transients. Hundreds of reefer compressors cycle simultaneously. And e-RTG crane VFDs draw 25–50% THD current.

What Causes It

Regeneration, Transients, and Reefer Chaos

STS cranes regenerate 5–10 kWh per cycle, 25–30 times per hour. That energy feeds back as voltage spikes. Cold ironing — increasingly mandated by EU AFIR — creates connection transients as ships plug in. And 2,000 reefer slots mean 2,000 compressors cycling on shared circuits.

The shore power mandate challenge + Read more− Close

EU AFIR mandates shore power at major ports by 2030. Each connection delivers 1–16 MVA through frequency converters that generate significant harmonic distortion. Without power quality management, ports face the double cost of infrastructure upgrades for capacity and harmonic mitigation. HarmoniQ addresses the power quality dimension, potentially reducing the scale of infrastructure investment needed.

Common Misconceptions

What We Hear — and the Reality
Myth
“Crane operations are inherently volatile — you can’t smooth that out.”
Reality
You can’t change the load profile of a crane cycle, but you can eliminate the reactive power waste and harmonic distortion it creates. HarmoniQ doesn’t slow your cranes down — it ensures every kWh they draw does useful work instead of heating cables and tripping equipment downstream.
Myth
“Shore power compliance is our only PQ concern — and that’s a 2030 problem.”
Reality
Shore power is coming, but your reefer compressors and crane VFDs are costing you millions right now. Power quality correction delivers immediate savings from existing loads while also preparing your infrastructure for shore power integration — reducing the total investment needed when the mandate arrives.
Myth
“Reefer temperature excursions are a refrigeration unit problem, not a power problem.”
Reality
Reefer compressor controllers are sensitive to voltage distortion. THD above 5% causes controller instability that leads to temperature cycling beyond setpoint. A single reefer cargo claim can exceed the entire cost of a HarmoniQ installation. Clean power means more stable reefer performance across all 2,000 slots.
