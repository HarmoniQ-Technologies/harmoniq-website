Power Quality for Hotels & Hospitality

The invisible electrical problem that’s inflating your energy bill, shortening equipment life, and quietly eroding your guest experience.

5 min read © 2026 HarmoniQ Technologies
← Back to Industries

The Problem

Power Quality Is a Guest Experience Issue

Hotels are uniquely sensitive to power quality. Unlike a factory, your guests feel the symptoms: flickering lights in ballrooms, audible transformer hum in quiet corridors, HVAC temperature swings, lift jerking, and electronic lock malfunctions. These issues erode satisfaction scores and repeat bookings — and most hotel teams never connect them to the electrical network.

Meanwhile, 8–18% of your electricity spend is being wasted as heat in cables, transformers, and motor windings, driven by the very equipment designed to make your property efficient: VFD-controlled HVAC, LED dimmers, commercial kitchens, laundry systems, and the growing fleet of EV chargers in your car park.

$65K–$80K
Annual electricity wasted through poor power quality in a typical 200-room hotel — with up to $150K more in reactive power penalties, equipment stress, and shortened asset life

What Causes It

Modern Hotels Are Harmonic-Rich Environments

Every VFD-controlled chiller, LED dimmer, switch-mode power supply, and EV charger draws current in non-linear pulses that distort the smooth 50/60 Hz sine wave. Hundreds of these loads operating simultaneously create harmonic distortion — electrical “noise” that wastes energy and stresses every component in your building’s electrical system.

EV charging — the growing power quality challenge + Read more− Close

EV charging is projected to reach 5–8% of hotel electrical load within 3–5 years. Each Level 2 charger draws 7–22 kW with 25–35% current THD from its rectifier. A hotel installing 10 chargers adds 70–220 kW of harmonic-rich demand. Without power quality management, this can push facility THD beyond compliance limits and trigger demand ratchet charges that persist for months after a single peak event.

Common Misconceptions

What We Hear — and the Reality
Myth
“We’re focused on guest experience, not electrical infrastructure.”
Reality
Power quality directly affects guest experience. LED dimmer flicker, HVAC temperature inconsistencies, lift jerking, and electronic lock faults are all symptoms of poor power quality. A single bad review mentioning flickering lights costs more than the fix.
Myth
“Our FM company manages all that.”
Reality
Most FM contracts cover maintenance response, not power quality optimisation. Ask your FM provider what your current THD and true power factor are — if they can’t answer, they’re not managing it. HarmoniQ works alongside FM providers and often gets specified into their scope after installation.
Myth
“We just completed a major refurbishment — our systems are new.”
Reality
New VFD-driven HVAC, LED lighting with dimmers, and modern kitchen equipment actually increase harmonic distortion. Post-refurbishment is often when power quality problems first appear. We frequently see hotels calling us 6–12 months after a refit when unexplained equipment failures start.
