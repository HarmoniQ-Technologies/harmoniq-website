Power Quality for Water & Utilities

How harmonic distortion from pumps and blowers is inflating energy costs, tripping SCADA systems, and shortening the life of critical infrastructure.

The Problem

Pumps, Blowers, and the Hidden Energy Drain

Water treatment and distribution is an energy-intensive business. Pumps, aeration blowers, and UV disinfection systems run continuously, and virtually all modern installations use VFDs for efficiency. But those VFDs create a hidden problem: harmonic distortion that wastes energy, damages equipment, and causes the nuisance SCADA trips that keep your team firefighting.

What Causes It

VFDs Save Energy but Create Harmonics

The 6-pulse rectifier front-end of a standard VFD draws current in sharp pulses, generating characteristic harmonics at the 5th, 7th, 11th, and 13th orders. With a dozen pumps and blowers on VFDs, these harmonics combine to create system-wide distortion that increases I²R losses, overheats motor windings, and interferes with sensitive SCADA and PLC equipment.

Why SCADA systems are particularly vulnerable + Read more− Close

SCADA controllers and PLCs are documented to experience logic faults when voltage THD exceeds 5%. At the 9.2% THDv typical of a VFD-heavy treatment works, communication errors on Modbus and Profibus networks cause intermittent sensor reading failures, control loop instability, and false alarms. These aren’t equipment faults — they’re power quality symptoms that disappear when THD is brought below 3%.
