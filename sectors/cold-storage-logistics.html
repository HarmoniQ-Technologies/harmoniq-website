Power Quality for Cold Storage & Logistics

Your compressors, blast freezers, and defrost cycles are wasting energy and shortening equipment life — and the problem is in your power, not your refrigeration.

The Problem

Cold Storage Is an Electrical Efficiency Problem

Cold storage facilities are dominated by VFD-driven compressors running continuously to maintain temperatures from +4°C to −25°C. These compressors are the biggest energy cost and the biggest source of harmonic distortion, generating 30–60% current THD that flows through your entire network.

What Causes It

Compressor Harmonics and Defrost Spikes

VFD-driven compressors draw current in sharp pulses generating 5th, 7th, 11th, and 13th harmonics. With multiple compressors on the same bus, voltage THD reaches 7–10%. Defrost cycles add 200–500 kW demand spikes 4–6 times daily, creating ratchet charges that persist for months.

Why temperature accuracy depends on power quality + Read more− Close

Cold chain compliance requires ±0.5°C accuracy. Harmonic distortion above 5% THDv causes measurement noise in temperature sensors and instability in electronic expansion valve controllers. The result is temperature fluctuations that trigger compliance alerts and force compressors to work harder — consuming more energy while delivering less precise control.

Common Misconceptions

What We Hear — and the Reality
Myth
“Our compressors are new and efficient — we don’t have a power quality issue.”
Reality
New, VFD-driven compressors are more efficient at converting power to cooling — but their VFD front-ends generate just as much harmonic distortion as older units. In fact, newer facilities with more VFDs often have worse THD than older ones with direct-on-line motors.
Myth
“Temperature compliance is about our refrigeration controls, not our power supply.”
Reality
Your refrigeration controls can only be as precise as the power feeding them. Voltage distortion above 5% causes expansion valve instability and sensor noise. If you’re seeing unexplained temperature excursions despite well-maintained refrigeration plant, power quality is almost certainly a contributing factor.
Myth
“We can’t risk any interruption to our cold chain for an installation.”
Reality
Neither can we — which is why HarmoniQ installs in parallel at the switchboard with zero impact on refrigeration circuits. No compressor shutdowns, no temperature excursions, no product risk. The system is designed specifically for environments where downtime is not an option.
