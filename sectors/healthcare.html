Power Quality for Healthcare

MRI scanners, operating theatres, and HVAC systems are creating power quality problems that affect imaging accuracy, equipment life, and your energy bill.

5 min read © 2026 HarmoniQ Technologies
← Back to Industries

The Problem

Hospitals Are Uniquely Vulnerable to Power Quality

Hospitals combine the most demanding harmonic-producing loads (MRI/CT scanners, VFD-driven HVAC, UPS systems) with the most sensitive equipment (surgical lighting, patient monitors, infusion pumps). The harmonic distortion from imaging equipment ripples through the entire network, affecting systems clinicians rely on for patient safety.

What Causes It

When Your Most Expensive Equipment Is the Problem

MRI and CT scanners are among the largest harmonic sources on any hospital network. Their gradient amplifiers draw large, rapidly varying currents. HVAC (40–60% of hospital load) adds VFD harmonics. UPS systems contribute rectifier harmonics. The equipment that needs the cleanest power is creating the most distortion for everything around it.

Operating theatre power quality + Read more− Close

Theatres require ultra-reliable power with precise environmental control. VFD-controlled AHUs maintain positive pressure cascades (15–25 Pa above corridors) for infection control. Voltage distortion above 5% causes imperceptible lighting flicker that fatigues surgeons, monitor display artefacts, and electrosurgical unit performance variations. HarmoniQ filtering maintains THDv below 2% at the theatre distribution board.
