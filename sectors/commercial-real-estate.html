The Problem

Modern Buildings Create Modern Power Problems

Commercial buildings have never been more electrically efficient on paper — VFD-controlled HVAC, LED lighting with smart dimmers, high-density IT loads, and EV charging infrastructure. But every one of these technologies creates harmonic distortion that wastes energy and degrades the very systems designed to save it.

The result: inflated electricity bills, shortened equipment life, lift ride-quality complaints, and — critically — a neutral conductor fire risk from triplen harmonics that building managers rarely know about.

What Causes It

The Triplen Harmonic Problem

LED drivers and switch-mode power supplies generate triplen harmonics (3rd, 9th, 15th order) that don’t cancel in the neutral conductor — they add. In buildings with extensive LED lighting, neutral current can reach 1.5–1.7x the phase current, overheating conductors sized for much lower loads. This is a genuine fire risk.

VFDs on HVAC and lifts add 5th and 7th harmonics. EV chargers contribute rectifier harmonics at 60–70% THD per charger.

Common Misconceptions

What We Hear — and the Reality
Myth
“Our building is only a few years old — power quality can’t be an issue.”
Reality
Newer buildings often have worse power quality because they’re packed with VFDs, LED dimmers, and EV chargers. Modern energy-efficient equipment is more efficient at its job, but generates more harmonic distortion as a side effect. The building’s age is irrelevant — its load profile determines its power quality.
Myth
“Our energy bill is small compared to rent — it’s not worth optimising.”
Reality
At a 6% cap rate, every $1 saved on operating costs adds $21 to property value. A $41,000 annual saving becomes a $685,000 valuation uplift. Add the EPC compliance benefit (proposed minimum B by 2030) and the fire risk reduction from neutral overloading, and this is an asset protection investment, not just an energy one.
Myth
“We can’t add EV chargers — our transformer is at capacity.”
Reality
Power quality correction typically frees 15–20% of transformer capacity by eliminating reactive power and harmonic losses. That’s often enough to add 10–20 EV chargers without a transformer upgrade — saving $127,000+ in infrastructure costs while meeting tenant and regulatory demands.
