Power Quality for Oil & Gas

SCR heaters, VFD compressors, and rectifier systems are costing your refinery millions in wasted energy — and putting safety systems at risk.

5 min read © 2026 HarmoniQ Technologies
← Back to Industries

The Problem

MV Process Loads Create LV-Wide Power Quality Chaos

Refineries and processing facilities are dense with harmonic-producing loads: SCR-controlled process heaters (1–20 MW each) at medium voltage, VFD-driven compressors, and large rectifier systems. The harmonic distortion from these MV loads propagates through step-down transformers to the low-voltage distribution network — MCCs, auxiliary pumps, instrument air compressors, building HVAC, control room power, and the hundreds of 480V motors that keep the refinery running.

The consequences go beyond energy waste. Voltage disturbances can trigger Emergency Shutdown (ESD) systems, causing unplanned shutdowns that cost hundreds of thousands per event.

What Causes It

SCR Loads and the Limits of Passive Filtering

SCR heaters fire at varying phase angles depending on process temperature demands, creating a harmonic spectrum that shifts continuously. Unlike the predictable harmonics from VFDs, SCR distortion changes from cycle to cycle — making tuned passive filters fundamentally inadequate.

ESD and fire & gas systems use sensitive relay logic and PLC-based controllers that can false-trigger on voltage transients. A single voltage sag during a large compressor start can propagate through the emergency shutdown hierarchy, causing a partial or full plant trip. The cost of a single unplanned shutdown is typically $635,000–$6,350,000. Clean power eliminates the voltage disturbances that trigger these events.

Common Misconceptions

What We Hear — and the Reality
Myth
“Our SCR heaters are individually within spec — we don’t have a harmonic problem.”
Reality
Individual SCR heaters may be within limits, but 20–50 operating simultaneously create aggregate distortion that far exceeds any single unit. The time-varying nature of SCR harmonics means they don’t cancel — they compound unpredictably across your network.
Myth
“We can’t install anything without a turnaround window.”
Reality
HarmoniQ connects in parallel at the main switchboard using independent breakers. No process circuits are interrupted. Installation happens during normal refinery operations with zero impact on production. No turnaround window needed.
Myth
“Our ESD trips are caused by instrumentation faults, not power quality.”
Reality
If ESD trips correlate with large motor starts or SCR firing events, they’re almost certainly voltage-transient related. Clean power typically eliminates the majority of “unexplained” safety system trips. The cost of even one prevented false trip often exceeds the entire HarmoniQ investment.
