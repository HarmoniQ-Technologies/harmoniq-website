Power Quality for Steel & Metals

The Problem

The Most Power-Hungry, Power-Hostile Sector

Steel and metals operations — particularly EAF mills — are the most electrically intensive and power-quality-challenged facilities in any industry. A single EAF draws 50–100 MW with a chaotic, continuously varying harmonic spectrum that no tuned filter can track. The arc’s negative resistance characteristic generates distortion across every harmonic order.

What Causes It

Arc Furnace Chaos and the Limits of SVCs

Unlike VFDs with predictable harmonics, EAFs generate a continuously varying spectrum that changes from cycle to cycle. During bore-down, maximum distortion with sub-harmonics. During refining, the spectrum shifts. Traditional SVCs address voltage flicker but don’t eliminate broadband harmonic distortion on downstream distribution.

Why tuned filters can’t solve this + Read more− Close

Tuned passive filters target specific harmonic orders. But the EAF spectrum is broadband and continuously varying — energy at every frequency, including interharmonics between standard orders. Passive filters also detune with temperature and ageing, and create resonance risk. Only active, software-controlled compensation can track and cancel a dynamic spectrum in real time. HarmoniQ responds in under 100 microseconds, continuously adapting.
