The Technology

HarmoniQ cleans the electrical current flowing through your network — eliminating harmonic distortion, reactive current, and phase imbalance at the source. With clean current, your equipment draws less electricity to do the same work. The energy that would have been wasted as heat in cables, motors, and transformers is simply never consumed. Unlike conventional power factor correction that only masks the problem at the meter, HarmoniQ corrects the current throughout your entire network.
Traditional capacitor banks correct power factor at the incoming feed — the meter point. This avoids utility penalties, but the dirty current still flows through every cable, motor, and transformer inside the network. Your equipment still draws distorted, reactive, unbalanced current — still wastes energy, still overheats, still ages prematurely. The meter looks better; the network does not. HarmoniQ is fundamentally different: it cleans the current across your entire network, so every piece of equipment downstream actually receives cleaner power, draws less current, and runs cooler. That is what delivers the 10–25% energy savings and 2–4× equipment life extension that meter-point correction alone cannot.

HarmoniQ is shunt-connected — it does not bear your facility's live load and is not in the power path. If the system is ever taken offline for any reason, your facility continues operating normally. You simply revert to the power quality and consumption levels you had before installation. There is no interruption risk, no explosion risk, and no safety hazard — unlike capacitor banks, which carry live load and can fail catastrophically.
The IEEE Arrhenius Law of Thermal Degradation shows that motor insulation life doubles for every 10°C reduction in operating temperature. HarmoniQ reduces equipment operating temperatures by up to 20°C — extending motor and transformer lifespan by 2–4×. This means a motor rated for 5–7 years could last its intended 20-year design life under HarmoniQ conditions. The maintenance cost reduction and capital deferral this creates often exceed the direct energy savings.

Installation & Operations

HarmoniQ is installed in parallel on the line side of your electrical network via spare breakers or fuse disconnects — meaning your operations continue uninterrupted throughout the entire process. Installation takes as little as 1 day for a single unit to 2 weeks for complex multi-panel sites. No rewiring or modifications to your existing equipment are required. Our certified engineers handle the full installation and commissioning as part of The HarmoniQ Pilot Protocol.
Following a site assessment and agreement, most installations are completed within 1–2 weeks. This includes equipment configuration and on-site commissioning. Simpler sites can be installed in as little as a single day. Larger or more complex multi-panel installations may take longer depending on site access and coordination requirements.
